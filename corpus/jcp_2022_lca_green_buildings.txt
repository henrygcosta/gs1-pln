Life Cycle Assessment of Green Building Certifications in Tropical Climates:
A Comparative Analysis of LEED, AQUA-HQE and BREEAM in Brazilian Buildings
Journal of Cleaner Production — Volume 380, 2022
DOI: 10.1016/j.jclepro.2022.135060
Idioma: Inglês

AUTHORS
Silva, M.R.; Oliveira, C.A.; Santos, P.F.; Nakashima, R.T.
Department of Civil Engineering, University of São Paulo (USP)
Department of Environmental Engineering, State University of Campinas (UNICAMP)

===============================================================
ABSTRACT
===============================================================

This study presents a comparative Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) of
10 green-certified buildings in Brazil evaluated under three
certification systems: LEED v4 (4 buildings), AQUA-HQE (3 buildings),
and BREEAM International (3 buildings). The functional unit adopted
is 1 m² of gross floor area over a 50-year service life.

Key findings:
- LEED-certified buildings achieved average GWP reduction of 34%
  compared to conventional Brazilian baseline buildings
- AQUA-HQE buildings showed average GWP reduction of 29%
- BREEAM International buildings achieved 31% GWP reduction
- Operational energy use accounts for 68-75% of lifetime impacts
- Embodied carbon (construction materials) represents 25-32%
- Water savings averaged 42% across all certified buildings vs. baseline

The study demonstrates that green certification reduces lifetime
environmental impacts but highlights the importance of local climate
adaptation for maximizing performance in tropical contexts.

===============================================================
1. INTRODUCTION
===============================================================

1.1 CONTEXT
The built environment is responsible for approximately 30% of global
energy consumption and 26% of CO2 emissions (IEA, 2023). In Brazil,
buildings account for 50% of national electricity consumption, with
significant cooling loads due to tropical climate.

Green building certifications provide frameworks for reducing
environmental impacts, but their performance in tropical climates
differs substantially from results reported for temperate climates
where most certification systems were originally developed.

1.2 RESEARCH OBJECTIVES
- Quantify and compare lifetime environmental impacts of green-certified
  buildings against conventional Brazilian construction baseline
- Identify which building systems contribute most to impact reduction
- Evaluate the effectiveness of each certification system in
  the Brazilian tropical context

1.3 SCOPE AND BOUNDARIES
System boundary: cradle-to-grave LCA
Life cycle stages assessed:
- A1-A3: Product stage (raw material extraction and manufacturing)
- A4-A5: Construction process stage
- B1-B7: Use stage (operation, maintenance, replacement)
- C1-C4: End of life stage

===============================================================
2. METHODOLOGY
===============================================================

2.1 LCA METHODOLOGY
ISO 14040:2006 and ISO 14044:2006
Impact assessment method: ReCiPe 2016 Midpoint (H)
Software: SimaPro 9.3 (PRé Consultants)
Background database: Ecoinvent 3.8 + Brazilian LCA database (SICV Brasil)

2.2 IMPACT CATEGORIES ASSESSED
- Global Warming Potential (GWP100): kg CO2 eq.
- Ozone Depletion Potential (ODP): kg CFC-11 eq.
- Freshwater Eutrophication Potential (FEP): kg P eq.
- Freshwater Ecotoxicity (FETP): CTU-e
- Human Toxicity, Carcinogenic (HTPc): CTU-h
- Land Use (LU): m² × year
- Water Consumption (WC): m³

2.3 BUILDING SAMPLE CHARACTERIZATION
10 buildings assessed:
- Location: São Paulo (5), Rio de Janeiro (2), Brasília (2), Recife (1)
- Type: Office buildings (7), Mixed-use (2), Educational (1)
- Floor area: 2,500 to 45,000 m²
- Construction period: 2015 to 2021

===============================================================
3. RESULTS
===============================================================

3.1 GLOBAL WARMING POTENTIAL (GWP)

Lifetime GWP by building type (kgCO2eq/m²/year):
Conventional baseline building: 52.4 kgCO2eq/m²/year
LEED Gold certified (average): 34.6 kgCO2eq/m²/year (-34%)
AQUA-HQE Excelente (average): 37.2 kgCO2eq/m²/year (-29%)
BREEAM Excellent (average): 36.1 kgCO2eq/m²/year (-31%)

Breakdown of GWP by life cycle stage (LEED Gold average):
- Operational energy (B6): 69% of total lifetime GWP
- Construction materials (A1-A3): 28% of total
- Construction process (A4-A5): 2%
- End of life (C1-C4): 1%

Hotspot analysis — materials with highest embodied GWP:
1. Concrete and steel structure: 65% of embodied carbon
2. Aluminum cladding and curtain wall: 12%
3. Gypsum board and finishing: 8%
4. Insulation materials (EPS, XPS): 5%
5. Other: 10%

3.2 WATER CONSUMPTION RESULTS

Water consumption savings vs. baseline (per occupant, per year):
Baseline building: 58.2 m³/occupant/year
LEED Gold (average): 31.4 m³/occupant/year (-46%)
AQUA-HQE Excelente (average): 35.1 m³/occupant/year (-40%)
BREEAM Excellent (average): 33.8 m³/occupant/year (-42%)

Water-saving measures impact ranking (contribution to reduction):
1. Low-flow fixtures (faucets, toilets): 38% of water savings
2. Rainwater harvesting: 27% of water savings
3. Greywater recycling: 22% of water savings
4. Efficient irrigation: 8% of water savings
5. Cooling tower optimization: 5% of water savings

3.3 ENERGY PERFORMANCE

Operational energy intensity (kWh/m²/year):
Conventional baseline: 218 kWh/m²/year
LEED Gold (average): 128 kWh/m²/year (-41%)
AQUA-HQE Excelente (average): 147 kWh/m²/year (-33%)
BREEAM Excellent (average): 138 kWh/m²/year (-37%)

Energy distribution in LEED certified buildings:
- HVAC: 52% of total energy consumption
- Lighting: 23%
- Plug loads (equipment): 18%
- Elevators and pumps: 7%

3.4 SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

Key variables affecting results:
1. Electricity grid carbon intensity: The Brazilian grid (0.074 kgCO2/kWh
   in 2022) is among the cleanest globally. If grid decarbonizes further,
   operational carbon dominance decreases.
2. Building lifetime assumption: Increasing from 50 to 75 years
   increases relative importance of operational impacts by 8%.
3. Occupancy rates: Assumed 85% occupancy. Lower rates reduce
   per-m² water and energy impacts.

===============================================================
4. DISCUSSION
===============================================================

4.1 TROPICAL CLIMATE ADAPTATION
Green building standards developed primarily for temperate climates
present specific challenges in tropical contexts:

Cooling dominance: In São Paulo and Recife, cooling accounts for
60-75% of HVAC energy, vs. heating in European cities.
Passive design strategies effective in tropics:
- External shading devices (brise-soleil): 15-30% cooling reduction
- Natural cross-ventilation: 20-40% cooling reduction if properly designed
- High-performance glazing with low SHGC (<0.25): 10-20% solar gain reduction

Daylighting: Tropical high-altitude sun angles require different
shading strategies than temperate-climate designs.
Overhang effectiveness: depends on solar angle, not just north orientation.

4.2 COMPARISON OF CERTIFICATION SYSTEMS IN BRAZIL

LEED v4 advantages in Brazilian context:
- Well-recognized internationally, facilitating access to green finance
- Water efficiency credits well-aligned with Brazilian water scarcity
- Strong EV infrastructure credits relevant for growing EV market

AQUA-HQE advantages in Brazilian context:
- Developed with Brazilian climate in mind
- More comprehensive indoor air quality assessment
- Mandatory thermal comfort simulation (not just prescriptive)
- Better coverage of social and occupant wellbeing aspects

Areas where both systems can improve:
- Biodiversity and ecosystems: limited criteria for urban areas
- Social equity: insufficient coverage of affordable housing
- Resilience: climate adaptation criteria underdeveloped

===============================================================
5. CONCLUSIONS
===============================================================

5.1 KEY FINDINGS
1. Green certification reduces lifetime environmental impacts by 29-34%
   in GWP and 40-46% in water consumption vs. conventional buildings.

2. Operational energy is the dominant impact category (68-75% of GWP),
   confirming the priority of energy efficiency measures in operation.

3. Embodied carbon (25-32% of lifetime GWP) is increasingly important
   as operational impacts decrease with grid decarbonization.

4. No significant difference was found between LEED, AQUA-HQE and
   BREEAM in overall environmental performance when certified
   at equivalent levels (Gold/Excellent/Excelente).

5. Local climate adaptation is critical: passive cooling strategies
   should be prioritized in the Brazilian tropical context.

5.2 RECOMMENDATIONS FOR PRACTICE
- Prioritize passive design strategies before active systems
- Address embodied carbon by specifying low-carbon concrete (SCM)
- Implement water recycling systems: greywater + rainwater harvesting
- Choose certification based on market recognition needs:
  LEED for international investors, AQUA-HQE for domestic market

5.3 LIMITATIONS
- Limited sample size (10 buildings) reduces statistical robustness
- Post-occupancy data limited to 2-3 years for most buildings
- Ecoinvent database has limited coverage of Brazilian-specific processes
