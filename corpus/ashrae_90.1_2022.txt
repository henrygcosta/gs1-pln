ANSI/ASHRAE/IES Standard 90.1-2022
Energy Standard for Sites and Buildings Except Low-Rise Residential Buildings
American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers (ASHRAE)
Atlanta, Georgia, USA — 2022 Edition
Idioma: Inglês

===============================================================
INTRODUCTION AND SCOPE
===============================================================

ASHRAE Standard 90.1 establishes the minimum energy efficiency
requirements for the design and construction of new buildings and
additions to existing buildings, except for low-rise residential
buildings (covered by ASHRAE Standard 90.2).

SCOPE:
- Commercial buildings: offices, retail, hotels, hospitals, schools
- Industrial buildings with conditioned spaces
- Buildings receiving federal funding or incentives
- Buildings seeking LEED certification (mandatory reference)

This standard is adopted by reference in the energy codes of all
50 US states and many international jurisdictions including Canada,
Mexico, and various countries.

EDITIONS: Standard updated approximately every 3 years.
2022 edition supersedes 2019 edition (most widely adopted globally).

===============================================================
CHAPTER 5 — BUILDING ENVELOPE
===============================================================

5.1 CLIMATE ZONES
ASHRAE 90.1 organizes requirements by climate zone (1 to 8):

Zone 1 — Very Hot: Miami, FL; Houston, TX; San Juan, PR
Zone 2 — Hot: Las Vegas, NV; Tampa, FL; New Orleans, LA
Zone 3 — Warm: Los Angeles, CA; Atlanta, GA; Dallas, TX
Zone 4 — Mixed: Seattle, WA; Baltimore, MD; Albuquerque, NM
Zone 5 — Cool: Chicago, IL; Denver, CO; Boston, MA
Zone 6 — Cold: Minneapolis, MN; Helena, MT; Burlington, VT
Zone 7 — Very Cold: Duluth, MN; International Falls, MN
Zone 8 — Subarctic: Fairbanks, AK; Barrow, AK

Subcategories:
A = Moist (precipitation > 635 mm/year)
B = Dry (precipitation ≤ 635 mm/year)
C = Marine (ratio of sensible solar heating ≥ 1)

5.2 ENVELOPE REQUIREMENTS — ROOFS

Maximum U-factor (W/m²K) for roofs:
                    Zone 1-2  Zone 3    Zone 4    Zone 5-6  Zone 7-8
Insulation above deck: 0.273   0.220    0.183     0.164     0.148
Metal building roof:   0.247   0.247    0.220     0.182     0.162

Alternative: Maximum assembly U-factor includes all components.
Solar Reflectance Index (SRI) requirements:
Low-slope roof (< 2:12 pitch): SRI ≥ 82 for Zones 1-3
Steep-slope roof (≥ 2:12 pitch): SRI ≥ 39 for Zones 1-3

5.3 ENVELOPE REQUIREMENTS — WALLS

Maximum U-factor for above-grade walls (W/m²K):
Mass walls (thermal mass ≥ 23.5 kg/m²):
Zone 1: U ≤ 0.701
Zone 2: U ≤ 0.513
Zone 3A & 3B: U ≤ 0.422
Zone 4 except Marine: U ≤ 0.326
Zone 5 & Marine: U ≤ 0.278
Zone 6: U ≤ 0.243
Zone 7: U ≤ 0.208
Zone 8: U ≤ 0.172

Steel-frame walls (with continuous insulation):
Zone 1: U ≤ 0.516
Zone 2: U ≤ 0.352
Zone 3: U ≤ 0.294
Zone 4: U ≤ 0.247
Zone 5: U ≤ 0.220
Zone 6: U ≤ 0.199
Zone 7: U ≤ 0.172
Zone 8: U ≤ 0.148

5.4 FENESTRATION REQUIREMENTS (Windows and Skylights)

SHGC (Solar Heat Gain Coefficient):
Measure of solar heat transmitted through glazing (0 = no gain, 1 = max gain).
Lower SHGC required in hot climates; higher allowed in cold climates.

Maximum SHGC for vertical fenestration (all orientations):
Zone 1: SHGC ≤ 0.25
Zone 2: SHGC ≤ 0.25
Zone 3: SHGC ≤ 0.25
Zone 4: SHGC ≤ 0.40
Zone 5: no requirement (PF adjustable)
Zone 6-8: no requirement

Maximum U-factor for vertical fenestration (W/m²K):
Zone 1: U ≤ 6.81
Zone 2: U ≤ 3.98
Zone 3: U ≤ 3.24
Zone 4: U ≤ 3.24
Zone 5: U ≤ 2.56
Zone 6: U ≤ 2.27
Zone 7: U ≤ 1.99
Zone 8: U ≤ 1.99

Maximum window-to-wall ratio (WWR):
No mandatory limit, but higher WWR requires lower U-factor and SHGC.
Trade-off compliance path allows compensations between parameters.

5.5 DOORS AND OPAQUE ASSEMBLIES

Opaque doors (non-fenestration): U ≤ 0.37 W/m²K for all zones.
Exception: Doors in conditioned spaces ≤ 0.67 m² and
doors primarily opaque may use wall requirements.

5.6 AIR LEAKAGE
Building envelope maximum air leakage:
0.2 L/s/m² at 75 Pa pressure difference (commercial buildings)
Tested by whole-building pressurization at project completion.

===============================================================
CHAPTER 6 — HVAC SYSTEMS
===============================================================

6.1 EQUIPMENT EFFICIENCY MINIMUMS

Unitary Air Conditioners and Heat Pumps — Cooling:
≤ 65,000 BTU/h: SEER2 ≥ 14.3
65,000-135,000 BTU/h: EER2 ≥ 11.5
135,000-240,000 BTU/h: EER2 ≥ 11.0
> 240,000 BTU/h: EER2 ≥ 10.0

Chillers (Water-cooled):
Centrifugal: IPLV ≥ 7.2 kW/kW (EER)
Positive Displacement: IPLV ≥ 6.3 kW/kW
Absorption: COP ≥ 0.72 (single effect), COP ≥ 1.22 (double effect)

Air-cooled chillers:
< 155 kW: EER ≥ 2.93
155 to 700 kW: EER ≥ 2.93
> 700 kW: EER ≥ 3.23

Heat pumps:
Air-source ≤ 19 kW: HSPF2 ≥ 7.5 (heating), SEER2 ≥ 14.3 (cooling)
Variable Refrigerant Flow (VRF): EER ≥ 3.3 (cooling), COP ≥ 3.3 (heating)

6.2 ECONOMIZER REQUIREMENTS
Air-side economizers required in most climate zones for systems > 9,400 L/s.
Exception: Systems using 100% outdoor air, or in zones where
economizer hours are insufficient (dry bulb or enthalpy controlled).

6.3 VENTILATION
Minimum ventilation per ASHRAE Standard 62.1-2019:
Office spaces: 2.5 L/s/person + 0.3 L/s/m²
Conference rooms: 2.5 L/s/person + 0.3 L/s/m²
Lobbies: 3.8 L/s/person + 0.3 L/s/m²
Classrooms: 5.0 L/s/person + 0.6 L/s/m²

Demand-controlled ventilation (DCV) required for:
Spaces > 25 persons with occupant density > 0.25 persons/m²
CO2 sensors as occupancy proxy are the most common DCV method.

6.4 ENERGY RECOVERY
Heat/energy recovery required when:
Outdoor air fraction ≥ 70% of supply air AND
System in climate zones with ≥ 750 hours at extremes per year.
Minimum effectiveness:
Sensible: 50% for supply and exhaust
Latent (energy recovery): 50%

===============================================================
CHAPTER 7 — SERVICE WATER HEATING
===============================================================

7.1 EQUIPMENT EFFICIENCY
Storage water heaters (electric resistance):
> 75 L: Uniform Energy Factor (UEF) ≥ 0.91

Heat pump water heaters (residential-type):
≤ 110 L: UEF ≥ 3.75
> 110 L: UEF ≥ 4.00

Gas-fired storage water heaters:
≤ 75 L: Energy Factor ≥ 0.67
> 75 L: Et ≥ 80%

7.2 PIPING INSULATION
All hot water supply pipes must be insulated:
Pipe diameter ≤ 25 mm: minimum 25 mm insulation
Pipe diameter 25-50 mm: minimum 38 mm insulation
Pipe diameter > 50 mm: minimum 50 mm insulation

7.3 SOLAR WATER HEATING
Not mandated by Standard 90.1, but may be credit toward
energy budget in performance path compliance.

===============================================================
CHAPTER 9 — LIGHTING
===============================================================

9.1 LIGHTING POWER DENSITY (LPD) — INTERIOR

Building Area Method (LPD in W/m²):
Office: 9.7 W/m²
Retail: 13.0 W/m²
Hospital: 11.3 W/m²
Hotel/motel: 7.6 W/m²
School/university: 9.7 W/m²
Warehouse: 7.0 W/m²
Restaurant: 12.0 W/m²
Convention center: 8.6 W/m²

9.2 LIGHTING CONTROLS
Automatic shutoff required in:
- All enclosed spaces ≤ 280 m²: occupancy sensor or automatic timer
- Open offices > 280 m²: daylight dimming in perimeter zones

Daylight zone controls:
Continuous dimming required in daylight zones ≤ 1.5 m from windows
and ≤ 3.0 m from skylights.

9.3 EXTERIOR LIGHTING
Lighting Power Allowance (LPA) limits for each exterior zone:
Zone 1 (dark sky): 0.5 W/m²
Zone 2 (rural): 1.0 W/m²
Zone 3 (suburban): 1.5 W/m²
Zone 4 (urban): 2.0 W/m²

Exterior luminaires ≥ 1,000 lumens: uplight fraction ≤ 0% for Zones 1-2.
All exterior luminaires ≥ 60 W: full cutoff required.

===============================================================
CHAPTER 11 — ENERGY COST BUDGET METHOD (ECB)
===============================================================

11.1 PERFORMANCE COMPLIANCE PATH
Alternative to prescriptive compliance.
Proposed design energy cost ≤ Budget building energy cost.

Budget building: same geometry as proposed, but using standard
systems and equipment meeting prescriptive requirements.
Simulation required: ASHRAE 90.1 Appendix G methodology.

Approved simulation software:
EnergyPlus, eQUEST, DOE-2, EnergyWizard, Trace 700, IES-VE
(must be validated per ASHRAE Standard 140)

11.2 EXCEPTIONAL CALCULATION METHODS
Allowed for innovative technologies not covered by Appendix G:
- Detailed daylighting analysis
- Natural ventilation modeling
- Phase-change material thermal storage
- Radiant heating/cooling systems
Requires documentation and peer review.

===============================================================
APPENDIX — NORMATIVE REQUIREMENTS
===============================================================

COMMISSIONING:
Fundamental commissioning of HVAC and lighting control systems
is required for all projects.
Enhanced commissioning required for projects seeking LEED certification.

ENERGY METERING:
Whole-building energy metering required for all new buildings.
Sub-metering required for each tenant space > 185 m².
Data must be retained for minimum 3 years.

ON-SITE RENEWABLE ENERGY:
Not required by 90.1, but additional compliance points available
through Appendix F (On-Site Renewable Energy).
PV or solar thermal generation reduces energy cost budget.

BUILDING AUTOMATION SYSTEMS (BAS):
Required for HVAC systems > 7,000 L/s supply air or > 100 kW cooling.
Must include:
- System-level control sequences
- Monitoring and diagnostics capability
- Integration with utility demand response programs
